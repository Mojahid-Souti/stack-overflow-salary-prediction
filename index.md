\# Can Experience Predict a Developer’s Salary?



\## Introduction

Software development is one of the fastest-growing careers worldwide, yet salaries

vary significantly across developers. Understanding what influences compensation

can help professionals plan their careers more effectively.



This project analyzes data from the Stack Overflow Developer Survey to explore how

professional coding experience relates to salary and whether experience can be used

to predict earnings.



\---



\## Questions Explored

1\. How does professional coding experience relate to salary?

2\. What does the distribution of developer salaries look like?

3\. Are developers with more experience more likely to earn above the median salary?

4\. Can a machine learning model predict whether a developer earns above the median salary?



\---



\## Key Findings

\*\*Experience and Salary Relationship\*\*  

Developers with more professional experience generally earn higher salaries.



\*\*Salary Distribution\*\*  

Compensation is right‑skewed, with a smaller group of developers earning significantly higher salaries.



\*\*Machine Learning Prediction\*\*  

A logistic regression model shows that experience alone provides meaningful predictive power.



\---



\## Conclusion

Professional experience is a strong factor in salary outcomes, though not the only one.

This analysis demonstrates how data science can provide valuable, real‑world insights

for career planning.
