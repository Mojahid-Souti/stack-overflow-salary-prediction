\# Can Experience Predict a Developer’s Salary?



\## Introduction

Software development is one of the fastest-growing careers worldwide, yet salaries

vary significantly across developers. Understanding what influences compensation

can help professionals plan their careers more effectively. Using data from the

Stack Overflow Developer Survey, this project explores how professional coding

experience relates to salary and whether experience can be used to predict earnings.



\---



\## Questions Explored

This project focuses on four main questions:

1\. How does professional coding experience relate to salary?

2\. What does the distribution of developer salaries look like?

3\. Are developers with more experience more likely to earn above the median salary?

4\. Can a machine learning model predict whether a developer earns above the median salary?



\---



\## Key Findings



\*\*Experience and Salary Relationship\*\*  

The analysis showed a clear positive relationship between years of professional

coding experience and salary. Developers with more experience generally earned

higher compensation than those earlier in their careers.



\*\*Salary Distribution\*\*  

Annual compensation was heavily skewed. While most developers earned salaries

close to the lower and middle ranges, a smaller group earned significantly higher

amounts, which raised the overall average. This made the median salary a more

reliable benchmark.



\*\*Machine Learning Prediction\*\*  

A logistic regression model was trained to predict whether a developer earns above

the median salary using years of professional experience. The model achieved

reasonable accuracy, indicating that experience is a strong predictor, even though

it does not capture all salary influences.



\---



\## Real‑World Scenario

To demonstrate practical use, the model was applied to a hypothetical developer

with five years of professional coding experience. Based on the model’s prediction,

the developer was classified as earning either above or below the median salary,

illustrating how data-driven insights can support career planning.



\---



\## Conclusion

This project demonstrates that professional coding experience plays an important

role in determining developer compensation. While salary is influenced by many

factors such as location, industry, and skills, experience alone provides valuable

insight. These findings highlight the importance of long-term skill development and

continuous learning in technology careers.
